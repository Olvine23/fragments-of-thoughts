---
title: Almost
date: December 2025
image: /images/almost.jpg
dedication: To the one whose belongings are still in my room
note: I thought I held love till I lost it. 
---

She was all that my fantasies described.
In my daydreams, and every night as I slept,
she matched the girl of my dreams—
completely, undeniably.
I’d never found a love so perfect
that my guts couldn’t hesitate
to crown her, embrace her,
call her… the one.
Well… almost.

Have you ever met someone
whose second name you immediately know—
not because she said it,
but because you’re certain
she’d one day share yours?
Because the future feels so aligned,
so destined,
that if she doesn’t take your name,
it feels like a missing piece in your story?
Someone whose sentences you finish,
not out of impatience,
but because your visions, ambitions, and dreams
are cut from the same cloth.
We were a team…
Well… almost.

Almost? No… not quite.
She was here.
Right there.
Where? Oooh… she’s gone.
A trial edition, perhaps?
No one warned me of the expiry.
Not just a trial—
a limited one,
where a subscription was possible
but not promised.
I found love…
and lost it in a glimpse.

How does one move on from that?
How do you recover
when all your sacred vows slip
through your fingers?
When your missing piece appears—
fits perfectly—
then vanishes,
leaving the space empty again?

Was it love I found…
or was it… almost?
